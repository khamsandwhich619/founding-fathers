4 Weird Facts about Alexander Hamilton

  1. The oldest unit in the United States Army can be traced back to Hamilton's artillery company during the revolutionary war.
    Battery D, 1st Batallion, 5th Field Artillery, 1st Infantry Division Mechanized is given the hononary legacy.

  2. George Washington's last letter before he died was an addressed reply to Hamilton concerning the idea of creating a "regular Millitary Academy" for the United States.
    Washington agreed and stated that it would be "of primary importance to this country".

  3. Alexander Hamilton was the original founder of the The New York Post. Established in 1801 as the The New York Evening Post,
    he concieved the publication to counter Jefferson's Federalist Party ideals.

  4. It is common knowledge among passionate history buffs that Alexander Hamilton was killed in a duel by Aaron Burr,
    but what is less common is that Alexander's own son was mortally wounded in a duel three years before his fathers duel.
    Alexander Hamilton was never the same after his son's death, and the very same pistols were used in his own duel between him and Aaron Burr.
