4 Weird Facts about Thomas Jefferson

  1. Thomas Jefferson loved to collect fossils. Jefferson was in possession of mammoth bones,
    and even the bones of a mastdon. You could say he was an early proto-archeologist.

  2. He was an avid architect. The Monticello, his famous home residing in the beautiful hills of Virginia,
    took Mr. Jefferson approximately 40 years to build. Additionally, he designed the iconic rotunda at the University of Virginia, and the Virginia State Capitol.

  3. Jefferson was an avid wine collector after spending a great deal of time in France on diplomacy trips.
    Monticello had two operating vineyards during his lifetime and he is recognized as one of America's first wine experts.

  4. Jefferson's personal library had over 6,500 books. After the Library of Congress was raided in the war of 1812,
    Jefferson offered his personal collection of books as a replacement.
